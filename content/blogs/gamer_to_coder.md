My first ever exposure to computers was when I was around 6th grade, playing GTA Vice City. Since then, I developed a consistency in completing my homeworks solely for the reason that I'd later be able to game on the computer without any worries. 

I would go on to play many such games, including IGI, Hitman, Need For Speed: Most Wanted - Black Edition, Euro Truck Simulator 2, COD Modern Warfare, Cities Skylines, Far Cry 3, and more.

In some of these games, I'd lose my patience, and would look for ways to cheat. You may know of Cheat Engine, a program that allowed you to edit a running game's variables. I was breaking the process isolation by running CE as administrator, overwriting the game process directly via memory, before I knew what memory even meant.

In other games, I'd experience lag. That brought me to [Linus Tech Tips](https://www.youtube.com/@LinusTechTips) and [Techquickie](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLQMVnqe4XbictUtFZK1-gBYvyUzTWJnOk) as I wanted to run games smoother.
